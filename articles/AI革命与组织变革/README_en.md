# AI Revolution and Organizational Change

## The Triple Symphony of the AI Revolution

### Movement 1: Technology — From Transformer to the Agent Era

In 2017, Google published "Attention Is All You You Need," introducing the Transformer architecture. The historical significance of this paper was almost unforeseen at the time: it wasn't just about better machine translation — it opened the era of **Large Language Models (LLM)**.

From GPT to Claude, from Gemini to Llama, from conversation to reasoning, LLMs' capabilities evolved from "toy" to "infrastructure" within just a few years. And in 2024-2025, the rise of **Agents** pushed this progression to a new stage: LLMs no longer just answer questions — they begin **acting on behalf of users** — calling tools, operating code, controlling other systems, completing task chains.

This mirrors the "railway moment" of the industrial revolution: railways didn't just make cargo transport faster, they redefined distance, economic geography, and industrial layout. Agents likewise don't just make task completion faster — they open the possibility of **"AI can do complete tasks for you"** — from "giving you advice" to "executing on your behalf."

### Movement 2: Politics — The Legitimacy Crisis of Algorithmic Power

Technology is never neutral. When AI gains the ability to influence employment, wealth distribution, and information flow, the political dimension becomes unavoidable.

**Several core contradictions are emerging:**

- **Data Sovereignty**: Who owns the data used to train AI? Individuals? Companies? Governments? When a model learns knowledge about you from your data, who does that knowledge belong to?
- **AI Sovereignty**: Is a nation's AI capability a "strategic asset"? When AI can influence elections, public opinion, and economic policy, how should nations seek autonomy in AI capability?
- **Wealth Distribution Fracture**: AI dramatically increases the productivity of knowledge workers, but a large number of mid-skill positions (clerical work, data entry, basic analysis) are being displaced. Where is the出路 for these people?
- **The Emergence of the "Useless Class"**: Historian Yuval Noah Harari提出：当AI可以在绝大多数认知任务上超过人类时，是否会形成一个庞大的"无用阶级" — their economic value approaches zero?

These are not technical questions. They are **political questions**, and they have no simple technical solutions.

### Movement 3: Organization — The Four Great Assumptions of Traditional Management Are Collapsing

For the past 100 years, management has been built on four fundamental assumptions:

1. **Knowledge resides in human brains**: the core of management is converting individual knowledge into organizational capability, hence the focus on hiring good people, training, and incentives;
2. **Execution requires supervision**: because knowledge is applied at the worksite, someone needs to monitor execution;
3. **Scale equals competitiveness**: large organizations have more resources and can better spread costs;
4. **Hierarchy is inevitable**: the pyramid structure is the most effective way to coordinate large-scale human organizations.

AI is overturning each of these assumptions:

- When LLMs can internalize and调用远超任何个人的知识时，"knowledge in human brains" fails;
- When AI can complete tasks without human supervision, "execution requires supervision" fails;
- When AI dramatically reduces the operating costs of small organizations, "scale equals competitiveness" fails;
- When organizations can rely on AI for coordination instead of层层审批, "hierarchy is inevitable" begins to动摇.

The result: **from pyramid to hive** — flatter, more distributed, more flexible organizational forms are becoming possible in the AI era.

## The New Class Map: Social Structure in the AI Era

The triple revolution is creating a new class structure.

This is not Marx's workers vs. capitalists — it is **a new stratification based on one's relationship with AI**:

| Class | Description | Relationship with AI |
|-------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Model Lords** | Companies and nations that control foundation models | Own the means of AI production |
| **Orchestrators** | Entrepreneurs who build products/platforms using AI capability | Use AI to amplify their own abilities |
| **Advanced Humans** | Professionals who collaborate with and harness AI | Partner with AI to multiply personal output |
| **AI Operators** | Practitioners who monitor, debug, and optimize AI systems | Operate AI but lack strategic perspective |
| **Physical Guardians** | People working in the physical world AI cannot cover | Guard the physical spaces AI cannot replace |
| **Useless Class** | People whose economic value has been replaced by AI and struggle to re-employment | Displaced by AI, lacking pathways to transition |

This structure is not static. Every class is moving up or down, and the speed of movement depends on: **the depth of your collaboration with AI, and the speed at which your organization adapts to new logic.**

## The CEO's "Napoleon Role": Rebuilding Order in an Institutional Vacuum

Napoleon appeared on the ruins of the French Revolution. He was not a revolutionary — he was **the one who re-established order after the revolution.**

The French Revolution of 1789-1799 destroyed the old regime but did not establish a new order. Chaos, ideological struggle, opportunism — a vacuum does not automatically produce an effective new structure. Napoleon used his military genius, legal system (Napoleonic Code), and administrative capability to turn chaos into a functioning national logic.

The AI revolution is creating a similar **institutional vacuum**:

- Old management logic is failing;
- New organizational logic is not yet clear;
- Chaos is not a policy vacuum — it is an opportunity vacuum;
- The CEO's position is not to "maintain the status quo" but **to design a new order on the ruins**.

This is not a comfortable position. It requires CEOs to simultaneously possess:
- Deep understanding of technology (not to write code, but to understand technology's boundaries and potential);
- Courage for organizational transformation (daring to build enclaves, daring to allow failure, daring to redefine interest structures);
- Commitment to human values (when AI can replace more and more work, what is the ultimate meaning of organization?).

## The "Napoleon Window": The Unique Opportunity for Chinese Enterprises

2026-2028 is a special time window. I call it the **"Napoleon Window"** — an era where institutional transformation and chaos coexist, and opportunity outweighs certainty.

Why is this window especially important for Chinese enterprises?

**Three reasons:**

1. **Late-mover advantage**: Western traditional enterprises have deeply entrenched interest structures under old logic, making transformation extremely costly; Chinese enterprises average younger with less historical baggage, making it easier to build AI-native organizational forms;
2. **Complete industrial ecosystem**: China possesses the world's most complete manufacturing ecosystem and most active consumer internet — AI has far richer落地场景 in this ecosystem than anywhere else;
3. **Supportive policy environment**: Sustained strong policy support for AI, rapidly improving computing infrastructure, data policies, and regulatory frameworks.

This means: **in China, the simultaneous occurrence of AI revolution and organizational transformation has better soil.**

## Touchstone Plan: New Ecosystem Position and Three-Layer Practice

Rainflower's "Touchstone Plan" was designed against this backdrop.

**Phase 1: Establish AI-Native Enclave (2025-2026)**
- Not trying to introduce AI into the main business, but establishing independently operated AI business units;
- Hiring, operating, and evaluating under new logic;
- Allowing failure, learning fast, building truly replicable AI organizational capability.

**Phase 2: Verify and Diffuse (2026-2027)**
- When the enclave validates a certain logic, gradually渗透 into the main business;
- Proving with data, persuading with cases, changing decisions with results;
- Establishing internal AI capability center to systematize enclave experience.

**Phase 3: Build Ecosystem Position (2027-2028)**
- Not pursuing our own efficiency, but thinking about how to make customers and partners succeed more easily on our AI capabilities;
- Through Rainflower's AI capabilities, helping customers establish their own AI transformation paths;
- Becoming the "node customers must pass through" on their AI journey.

## Conclusion: Finding Your Position in the Triple Revolution

The AI revolution is not a single-dimensional technology upgrade — it is the simultaneous compression of technological, political, and organizational transformation. This makes it faster, deeper, and more unpredictable than any previous technological revolution.

But this does not mean we can only passively wait.

**The question every organization needs to answer:**
- Is the fundamental logic of your industry being rewritten by AI?
- Is your organization preparing for that new logic?
- When is your "Napoleon Window"?

The answer won't appear automatically. Only organizations that actively seek answers in chaos will have the opportunity to stand in the right place when the new order is established.

This is not a comfortable era. But it is an era that belongs to builders.
